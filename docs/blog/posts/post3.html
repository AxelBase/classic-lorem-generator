How to Generate Perfectly Balanced Paragraphs Every Time

Published: November 24, 2025

Not all placeholder text is created equal. A 200-word paragraph looks very different from a 60-word one — and choosing the right length can make or break your layout.

This tool gives you four precision length modes. Here’s exactly when (and why) to use each:

Short (~50–80 words)

Perfect for:

  • Hero sections
  • Card descriptions
  • Testimonials
  • Mobile app screens

Keeps blocks tight and scannable.

Medium (~80–120 words)

The goldilocks zone. Use for:

  • Blog preview cards
  • Feature sections
  • About pages
  • Most body content

Default for a reason — looks natural everywhere.

Long (~120–180 words)

Ideal for:

  • Article pages
  • Case studies
  • Landing page sections
  • Print layout mockups

Tests real reading flow and line length.

Random (no limit)

Use when you want true variety — perfect for testing responsive grids or dynamic content areas.

Pro tip: Combine “real first paragraph” with Medium or Long for the most authentic article mockups.

Great design isn’t about filling space — it’s about filling it right.
